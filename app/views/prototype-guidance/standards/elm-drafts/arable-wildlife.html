Sustainable Farming Incentive (SFI) standard: arable wildlife

The environmental standard for arable wildlife under the SFI scheme, who can do it, what you need to do and how much you can get paid.

Published 29 October 2020
From: Defra

What the standard for arable wildlife is

Use this standard to increase habitat, including food, shelter and nesting cover for wildlife on farms all year round. By doing this you can:

  • protect biodiversity against the effects of climate change by providing shelter and wildlife corridors
  • manage pests by providing food and shelter for beneficial predators
  • improve soil condition and carbon storage by adding organic matter
  • protect water bodies from runoff containing sediment and pesticides
  • improve the character of landscape by softening large blocks of arable land

How much land managers can get paid for completing the standard.

Eligibility

You can choose this standard if you have arable or temporary grassland.

Choose intermediate or advanced levels of the standard if your land is especially important for:

  • farmland birds or pollinators
  • rare arable plants

Do not choose this standard:

  • for high cover wildflowers
  • on fields where there is a high risk of erosion as overwintered stubbles maintains bare ground

How to complete the arable wildlife standard

You need to give over at least 5% of the farmed area to provide food, shelter and nesting cover for farmland birds and pollinating insects all year round.

To do this, provide:

  • 1% for nesting and shelter for birds
  • 2% for flower-rich habitat
  • 2% for winter seed for birds

Choose how you want to feed birds:

  • leaving the stubble of previous crops over winter and planting the next crop in spring
  • creating flower-rich grass margins and plots
  • not harvesting cereal headlands

If you have ditches, clear vegetation in channels on a rotation of at least 3 years, and cut no more than half the length of bank vegetation in any year.

Keep a record of:

  • record 1
  • record 2

To help you complete the standard, read:

  • Create and maintain buffer strips around high priority features
  • Ensure there is a range of habitats to provide year-round resources for invertebrates
  • Create and maintain legume or nectar and flower- rich margins or plots
  • Create areas of bare ground for invertebrates and pollinating insects
  • Create and maintain pollen and nectar plots
