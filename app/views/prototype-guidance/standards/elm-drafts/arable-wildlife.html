Environmental aims of this standard

The aim of this standard is to increase habitat for wildlife, including food, shelter and nesting cover, on your farm. The standard also helps to:

  • manage pests by providing food and shelter for predators
  • improve soil condition and carbon storage
  • protect watercourses from runoff
  • increase wildlife resistance to climate change by providing shelter and wildlife corridors
  • strengthen the character of landscape by softening large blocks of arable land

Eligibility

You can choose this standard if you have arable or temporary grassland.

Choose intermediate or advanced levels of the standard if data shows your land is especially important for:

  • farmland birds or pollinators
  • rare arable plants

Do not use this standard:

  • for high cover of wildflower or bird food
  • on fields where there is a high risk of erosion as overwintered stubbles maintains bare ground

How to complete this standard

Instructions for each delivery level

Basic level:

  • Use at least 5% of your farmed area as a year-round resource for farmland birds and pollinating insects.
  • Provide this as 1% nesting and cover, 2% insect and flower-rich habitat, and 2% winter seed food.
  • Choose from:
    • basic overwintered stubble
    • sown winter bird food
    • floristically enhanced grass margins and plots
    • unharvested cereal headlands
  • Where you have ditches:
    • carry out in-channel management on a rotation of at least 3 years
    • cut no more than half the length of bank vegetation in any year

Intermediate level:

  • Use at least 8% of your farmed area as a year-round resource for farmland birds and pollinating insects.
  • Provide this as 2% nesting and cover, 3% insect and flower-rich habitat, and 3% winter seed food.
  • Include at least one of the following, in addition to baseline actions:
    • enhanced overwintered stubbles
    • cultivated fallow plots for arable plants and ground nesting birds
    • creation/ management/ enhancement of beetle banks
    • sown legume fallows
    • supplementary winter bird feeding

Advanced level:

  • At least 10% of the farmed area managed to provide year-round resources for farmland birds and pollinating insects
  • Provide this as 2% nesting & cover, 4% insect & flower-rich habitat and 4% winter seed food
  • Additional measures above the intermediate level should include:
    • creation of areas of scrub and tall vegetation (nesting, shelter) through natural regeneration
    • buffer ponds and manage margins to retain existing scrub, or allow scrub to develop on up to 30% of the margin
