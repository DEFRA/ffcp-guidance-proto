Farmland bird management

A good farmland bird habitat should include:

  • access to winter food
  • spring food suitable for chicks and adults
  • somewhere for birds to nest

Identify birds on your land

You can use local data sources to identify which birds are, or could be, on your land. Speak to an environmental land management (ELM) adviser to find out if any landscape-scale initiatives, such as farmer clusters, are targeting birds in your area.

Each type of bird will need specific management. Some birds will need more management than others. Learn about different types of birds.

Identify bird habitats on your land

You may have several different species of farmland bird on your land and in the nearby landscape. Some species can be found in more than one type of habitat.

Farmland bird habitats include:

  • arable farmland
  • grassland farmland
  • lowland wet grasslands
  • upland in-bye grasslands

Each type of bird habitat will need specific management. Some habitats will need more management than others. Find out more about different types of bird habitat.

Decide which birds you want to manage

To choose what bird species you will manage, you should think about:

  • which birds are currently on your land
  • if you want to attract specific birds to your land
  • if you can do the required management activities

To help you decide, you can speak to an adviser, find guidance on specific bird species, or find a bird organisation with experience of the birds and habitats you are interested in.
