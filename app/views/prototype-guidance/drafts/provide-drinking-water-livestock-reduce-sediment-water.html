Guidance

U3: Provide drinking water for livestock to reduce the amount of sediment in water bodies

Find out how to reduce sediment in water bodies by providing livestock with troughs, pasture pumps, ram pumps, and associated pipework.

Published 26 February 2020
From: Natural England

  3. Install a pasture pump

How this will benefit the environment

If you provide livestock with an alternative to drinking from watercourses and ponds, it can reduce:

  • bank erosion
  • sediment pollution
  • the faecal contamination of watercourses

Install a trough and associated pipework

Place the trough:

  • on hard-standing or on well-drained soil to avoid unnecessary trampling (poaching), erosion or runoff
  • away from features through which organic manures and sediment could enter waterways

Troughs should not:

  • allow overflow or leaks to contribute to runoff, ponding or erosion in the field
  • damage historic or environmental features identified in the HEFER

Do not lay pipes:

  • under housing or roof structures
  • within 10m of watercourses
  • on areas vulnerable to soil erosion as identified on the Farm Environment Record (FER)
  • within 6m of historic or archaeological features identified in the HEFER

Install a pasture pump

Pasture pumps can lift water up to around 5m and pump to a distance of up to 50m. One pump can serve 10 to 15 cattle.

How to get the best results from the pump

It may be necessary to train livestock to use the pump.

What to do with the pump in the winter

  • remove the pump and store it during the winter to guard against frost damage
  • make sure the pump is available for inspection after it is removed

Install ram pumps

A ram pump uses the power of flowing water to pump a smaller quantity of water at a much higher pressure. Ram pumps do not need fuel or electricity as they run entirely on water. Use a ram pump to pump fresh water from a stream to a head tank or reservoir.

Where to install the pump

Install the pump on a site with enough hydraulic head and a consistent water supply throughout the year.

How to operate the pump

Ram pumps can pump over large distances, but they need a buffer tank at the top of the system. The water entering the pump must have a low sediment content, or the resulting abrasion will lead to excessive wear and tear. Reduce the amount of sediment by using a sedimentation tank.

Keeping records

We may ask you for:

  • any consents or permissions connected with the work
  • receipted invoices, or bank statements
  • photographs of site before works start

You should keep:

  • photographs of the site during the different stages of construction or contracts
  • invoices or other documents confirming the technical specification for the completed works
  • photographs of the completed works in place and installed
