Guidance

U2: Fence off water sources from livestock to prevent erosion and contamination

Find out how to fence off rivers, streams, lakes and ponds from livestock to protect environmental features and help manage habitats.

Published 26 February 2020
From: Natural England

Contents

  1. How this will benefit the environment
  2. Advice and suggestions on how to put up fences and sheep netting
  3. Keeping records
  4. Use this action for Environmental Land Management (ELM)

How this will benefit the environment

Fences and sheep netting will help control livestock and:

  • prevent bankside erosion
  • reduce nutrient input
  • reduce faecal contamination

Advice and suggestions on how to put up fences and sheep netting

You should not:

  • attach the fence or netting to trees or hedgerows
  • block or restrict access to open access land

Keeping records

We may ask you for:

  • any consents or permissions connected with the work
  • receipted invoices, or bank statements
  • photographs of the site before works start

You should keep:

  • photographs of completed work
